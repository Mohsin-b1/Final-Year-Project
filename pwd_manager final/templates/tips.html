Password Security Tips Content

A 2019 Study by Google and Harris showed that users struggle to keep track of their passwords



Tip #1:

Do not use easy to guess passwords they are the most common! Passwords like 123456, password, or abc123 are not secure and are easily guess create difficult to guess strong passwords.

Tip #2:

Change your password after it has been involved in a breach If you know your password has been breached then you should change that password for all accounts that use it so as not to get other accounts compromised.

Tip #3:

Do not use the same password If you use the same password for all accounts you have one breach could mean the compromise of all or multiple of your accounts

Tip #4:

Create a complex password with more than 8 characters Shorter passwords are easier to crack. Did you know hackers can use specialised dictionaries to crack weak passwords and gain access to your account.

Tip #5:

Use a password manager It can be difficult to keep track of multiple strong and long passwords so to make it easier make use of a password manager that allows you to keep track of them in one place.

Tip #6:

Use 2FA Two Factor Authentication allows you to verify yourself using your mobile device and provides an extra layer of security.

Tip #7:

Do not write passwords down on paper, or save anywhere on unencrypted files Writing passwords down is never a good Idea, it is a very unsecure habit and can potentially get your accounts compromised.

Tip #8:

Watch out for Phishing Make yourself Cyber aware and watch out for phishing emails or links. Hackers may attempt to trick you into installing software like keyloggers which can monitor your keystrokes and attempt to get you to reveal your password.
